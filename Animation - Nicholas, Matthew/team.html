TEAM

The Golden State Warriors were founded in Philadelphia in the year 1946 and were first called the Philadelphia warriors. They were originally owned by Peter A. Tyrrell who was an American entertainment entrepreneur and also one of the eleven co-founders of the BAA which eventually became the NBA (National Basketball Association). He was also the owner of the Philadelphia Rockets at the time. Eddie Gottlieb was hired as the team first coach and general manager. In the 1962 season Frank Mieuli purchased the team and relocated them to the San Francisco bay area and were renamed the San Francisco Warriors. Then in the 1971-1972 season the team changed their name again to the Golden State Warriors after playing all home games in San Diego. The Golden State Warriors still remain the only NBA team to not include their state or city in the name of their team. Throughout the 1980s the team struggled to to make a competitive team and make the finals. Now in the 21st century after their resurgence the Golden State Warriors are the current 2017 NBA champions.
